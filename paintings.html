Paintings

I believe a good painting does not become boring, no matter how many times/years you look at it. In fact, as you grow your appreciation of it grows. Every time you glance at it, you discover new mysteries and as it nourishes your spirit, it soothes your mood and spurs your imagination. Before publishing any work, I sit and stare at it for hours and publish it only if it meets the standards described above. If not, I discard them.

Incomplete Circles

This painting is a reflection of our lives. In our lifetime, we embark on many journeys (create many circles) but we don't finish all of them. The one we usually finish is the smallest one. Some of our initiatives are big and some small. Some we start from the wrong spot some at the wrong time. And yet, some are even bigger than the frame of our life. But the overall beauty persists with all the shades and colors we bring to our lives.

Acrylic on Canvas 30" x 48"

Captivating Beauty

This painting is inspired by nature. In the background, there are different shades of green that resemble our true colors. On that level, we are all the same. We are all different shades of green. However, on a superficial level, we are different, which must not prevent us from attaining our true reality. There are black lines that resemble our share of sorrow in life, white lines resemble our share of good fortunes and happiness and the golden lines resemble our accomplishments, education, and so on. We have to understand that for it to look beautiful all of these colors have to come together in harmony.

Autumn Leaf

Autumn (fall season) has always been a source of fascination for me. Autumn is the season of death or transformation for so many leaves. At their graceful departure, these leaves, create a magnificent beauty and wants us to remember a great image so that we impatiently await their return with great anticipation in the next spring. There is so much that we can learn from the falling leaves.

Acrylic on canvas 48"x48"
